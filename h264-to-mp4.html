About H264

H264, also known as AVC, is a widely used video compression standard designed to deliver good visual quality at relatively efficient bitrates. An H264 file is often produced by cameras, recorders, ripping tools, transcoders, and surveillance systems. In some workflows, the output is saved as a raw `.h264` stream instead of a finished container file, which can make playback less convenient in everyday apps.

That is why an H264 to MP4 converter is useful. The video data may already be H264-compressed, but wrapping the stream in MP4 makes the file easier to use with media players, browsers, mobile devices, editors, and upload workflows. For users who need a quick online converter, converting H264 to MP4 is often the fastest compatibility fix.

About MP4

MP4 is one of the most common video container formats in modern media workflows. MP4 can package video, audio, subtitles, and metadata into a single file that works well across Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iPhone, browsers, editing apps, and publishing platforms. That broad support is a major reason people use an MP4 converter when the original source is stored in a less convenient form.

MP4 is also a practical format for delivery and streaming. A well-formed MP4 is easier to preview, easier to share with teammates, and easier to upload to websites and content systems. This H264 to MP4 converter focuses on creating an MP4 file that is more compatible for day-to-day viewing while keeping the workflow free, online, and private.
