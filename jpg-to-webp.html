About JPG

JPG, also called JPEG, is one of the most common image formats on the web. JPG uses lossy compression to shrink file size, which makes JPG ideal for photos, banners, social media graphics, and website images. A JPG file is widely supported across browsers, phones, design tools, and content systems, so JPG remains a standard source format before you convert JPG to WEBP.

Even though JPG is popular, JPG files are often larger than modern web formats at similar visual quality. That is why many teams use a JPG to WEBP converter after exporting JPG assets. Converting JPG to WEBP can preserve the look of the original image while reducing transfer size, which is useful for SEO, Core Web Vitals, and better loading speed.

About WEBP

WEBP is a modern image format developed by Google for efficient web delivery. WEBP supports lossy and lossless compression, which means WEBP can handle photographic images, interface graphics, screenshots, and transparent assets in a compact format. A WEBP file is designed to keep images sharp while reducing file size, making WEBP a practical choice for websites, apps, ecommerce, and marketing pages.

Many developers and site owners use a WEBP converter because WEBP images typically load faster than older formats. When you convert JPG to WEBP, you often get smaller output files with similar visible quality. That makes this JPG to WEBP converter useful when you want an online WEBP converter that improves page speed, saves bandwidth, and supports modern browser experiences.
